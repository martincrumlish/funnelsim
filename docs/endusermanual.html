1 Getting Started

Creating Your Account

  1. Click "Get Started" or "Sign Up" on the landing page
  2. Enter your email address and create a password
  3. Check your email for a verification link (if required)
  4. Once verified, you'll be taken to your dashboard

Signing In

If you already have an account:

  1. Click "Sign In"
  2. Enter your email and password
  3. Click "Sign In" to access your dashboard

Resetting Your Password

Forgot your password? No problem:

  1. Click "Forgot password?" on the sign-in page
  2. Enter your email address
  3. Check your email for a reset link
  4. Click the link and enter your new password
Your Account

Your plan determines how many funnels you can create. Check your Profile page to see your current limits and upgrade options.

2 Your Dashboard

The dashboard is your home base. Here you can see all your funnels at a glance.

Dashboard Features

  • Funnel Cards - Each funnel appears as a card showing its name and last modified date
  • Create New Funnel - Click the "+" button or "New Funnel" to start fresh
  • Search & Filter - Quickly find funnels by name
  • Funnel Usage - See how many funnels you've used vs. your plan limit

Managing Funnels

Click on any funnel card to open it in the builder. To delete a funnel, click the menu icon on the card and select "Delete".

Deletion is Permanent

Deleted funnels cannot be recovered. Make sure to export your funnel before deleting if you want to keep a copy.

3 Creating Funnels

Start a New Funnel

  1. From your dashboard, click "New Funnel" or the "+" button
  2. Enter a name for your funnel (e.g., "Product Launch Funnel")
  3. Click "Create"
  4. You'll be taken to the funnel builder with a blank canvas

Renaming a Funnel

Click on the funnel name at the top of the builder to edit it. Press Enter or click away to save.

Auto-Save

Your work is automatically saved as you make changes. You'll see a brief "Saving..." indicator when changes are being saved.

Never Lose Work

All changes are saved to the cloud automatically. You can close your browser and come back later - your funnel will be exactly as you left it.

4 Understanding Node Types

Funnels are built from nodes (steps) connected together. Each node type serves a specific purpose in your sales flow.

Frontend Frontend Offer

The first offer your visitors see. This is typically your main product or lead magnet.

  • Entry point for all traffic
  • Usually your lowest-priced or free offer
  • Goal: Convert cold traffic into buyers

OTO One-Time Offer (Upsell)

An upsell shown to people who purchased the previous offer. Only buyers see this.

  • Higher-priced complementary product
  • Shown immediately after a purchase
  • Goal: Increase average order value

Downsell Downsell Offer

A lower-priced alternative shown when someone declines an offer.

  • Cheaper version or payment plan
  • Shown when visitor says "No" to an upsell
  • Goal: Capture sales you would have lost

Node Settings

Each node has configurable settings:

Setting Description
Name Label for this step (e.g., "Main Course", "VIP Upgrade")
Price The price of this offer in dollars
Conversion Rate Expected percentage of visitors who will buy (0-100%)

5 Building Your Funnel

Adding Nodes

  1. Click the "Add Node" button or right-click on the canvas
  2. Select the node type (Frontend, OTO, or Downsell)
  3. The new node appears on the canvas
  4. Drag it to your desired position

Connecting Nodes

Nodes have connection points (handles) for creating flow:

  • Yes (Green) - Connect to the next step for buyers
  • No (Red) - Connect to the next step for non-buyers

To connect nodes:

  1. Click and drag from a handle (Yes or No) on one node
  2. Drop onto the input handle of another node
  3. A line appears showing the connection

Editing Nodes

Click on any node to select it and edit its settings in the side panel:

  • Change the name, price, and conversion rate
  • See real-time updates to your metrics

Deleting Nodes

Select a node and press Delete or Backspace, or click the delete icon in the node settings.

Deleting Connections

Click on a connection line to select it, then press Delete or Backspace.

Pro Tip: Typical Funnel Structure

A common high-converting structure is: Frontend → OTO 1 → Downsell → OTO 2. The frontend captures initial buyers, OTO 1 offers an upgrade, the downsell catches those who decline, and OTO 2 offers another upgrade opportunity.

Canvas Navigation

  • Pan - Click and drag on empty canvas space
  • Zoom - Use mouse scroll wheel or pinch on trackpad
  • Fit View - Click the "Fit" button to see all nodes

6 Traffic Sources

Traffic sources define where your visitors come from and how much you're paying to acquire them.

Adding a Traffic Source

  1. Find the "Traffic Sources" panel (usually on the left or in a collapsible menu)
  2. Click "Add Traffic Source"
  3. Enter the source details:
    • Name - e.g., "Facebook Ads", "Google Ads", "Email List"
    • Visitors - Number of visitors from this source
    • Cost - Total ad spend for this traffic

Multiple Traffic Sources

You can add multiple traffic sources to simulate blended traffic. The app will combine them and show aggregate metrics.

Source Visitors Cost Cost Per Visitor
Facebook Ads 1,000 $500 $0.50
Google Ads 500 $400 $0.80
Email List 2,000 $0 $0.00
Total 3,500 $900 $0.26
Free Traffic

For organic traffic (SEO, social media, email list), set the cost to $0. This helps you see the true profitability of paid vs. free traffic.

7 Understanding Metrics

The app calculates several key metrics to help you understand your funnel's performance.

Key Metrics Explained

Metric What It Means
Total Revenue Total money generated from all offers in your funnel
Total Profit Revenue minus traffic costs (what you actually keep)
EPC (Earnings Per Click) Average revenue per visitor. If EPC > Cost Per Click, you're profitable.
ROI Return on investment: (Profit / Cost) × 100%
Conversion Rate Percentage of visitors who buy at each step

Per-Node Metrics

Each node shows its own metrics:

  • Traffic - Number of visitors reaching this step
  • Buyers - Number who purchase (Traffic × Conversion Rate)
  • Revenue - Money generated (Buyers × Price)

Revenue Badges

Nodes display floating badges showing revenue generated:

  • Green - Profitable step
  • Red - Needs optimization

Sensitivity Analysis

Hover over any node to see how a 1% increase in conversion rate would affect your total profit. This helps you identify which steps have the biggest impact.

Pro Tip: Focus on High-Impact Steps

Use sensitivity analysis to find where small improvements yield the biggest returns. Often, improving your frontend conversion by 1% has more impact than improving a downsell by 5%.

8 Optimization Tips

Benchmark Conversion Rates

Use these as starting points, then adjust based on your actual data:

Offer Type Low Average Good
Free Lead Magnet 20% 35% 50%+
Low-Ticket Frontend ($7-47) 1% 3% 5%+
OTO/Upsell 10% 20% 30%+
Downsell 5% 15% 25%+
High-Ticket ($500+) 0.5% 1% 2%+

Profitable Funnel Checklist

Your Funnel is Healthy When:
  • EPC is higher than your Cost Per Click
  • Total Profit is positive (green)
  • Each upsell adds meaningful revenue
  • Downsells are capturing lost sales

Common Optimization Strategies

  1. Lower frontend price - Get more buyers into your funnel, make profit on backend
  2. Add an order bump - Small add-on offer on checkout page (model as part of frontend)
  3. Test higher OTO prices - Buyers are in buying mode; don't leave money on the table
  4. Add a downsell - Capture 10-25% of people who say no to your upsell
  5. Reduce traffic cost - Better targeting, better ads, or organic traffic

Scenario Testing

Use the app to test "what if" scenarios:

  • What if I raised my frontend price by $10?
  • What if my conversion rate improved by 2%?
  • What if I added another upsell?
  • What if my ad costs doubled?

Simply adjust the values and watch the metrics update in real-time.

9 Exporting Your Funnel

Share your funnel designs with team members, clients, or for your own records.

Export as Image (PNG)

  1. Click the "Export" button in the toolbar
  2. Select "PNG Image"
  3. Your funnel is downloaded as a high-resolution image

Best for: Quick sharing, presentations, social media

Export as PDF

  1. Click the "Export" button in the toolbar
  2. Select "PDF Report"
  3. A professional PDF is generated with your funnel diagram and metrics

Best for: Client presentations, documentation, printing

Sharing with Clients

PDF exports are perfect for presenting funnel strategies to clients. They include the visual flow and key metrics in a professional format.

10 Managing Your Subscription

Viewing Your Plan

Go to your Profile page to see:

  • Your current plan
  • Funnel usage (how many you've created vs. your limit)
  • Next billing date (for paid subscriptions)

Plan Limits

Different plans offer different funnel limits and features. Visit the Pricing page or your Profile to see available plans and what's included in each.

Upgrading Your Plan

  1. Go to your Profile page
  2. Click "Upgrade" on your desired plan
  3. Choose billing frequency (Monthly, Yearly, or Lifetime)
  4. Complete checkout securely via Stripe
  5. Your new limits are available immediately

Managing Billing

Click "Manage Subscription" to open the billing portal where you can:

  • Update your payment method
  • View billing history and invoices
  • Change your plan
  • Cancel your subscription
Lifetime Plans

If you purchased a lifetime plan, you have permanent access with no recurring billing. Your profile will show a "Lifetime" badge instead of a renewal date.

11 Frequently Asked Questions

Can I edit a funnel after creating it?

Yes! Click on any funnel from your dashboard to open it. All changes are saved automatically.

What happens if I reach my funnel limit?

You'll see a prompt to upgrade your plan (if higher tiers are available). Your existing funnels remain accessible - you just can't create new ones until you upgrade or delete an existing funnel.

Are my funnels private?

Yes. Only you can see your funnels. They're stored securely in the cloud and tied to your account.

Can I share a funnel with someone else?

You can export your funnel as a PNG or PDF to share. Direct collaboration features (sharing edit access) may be available in future updates.

How accurate are the projections?

The calculations are mathematically precise based on the numbers you input. The accuracy of projections depends on how realistic your conversion rate estimates are. Start with industry benchmarks, then refine based on real data.

Can I duplicate a funnel?

This feature is coming soon. For now, you can create a new funnel and manually recreate the structure.

What if I cancel my subscription?

Your account reverts to the base plan at the end of your billing period. If you have more funnels than allowed on that plan, additional funnels become read-only until you upgrade again or delete funnels to get under the limit.

How do I delete my account?

Contact support to request account deletion. This will permanently remove all your data.

Is there a mobile app?

The app is web-based and works in any modern browser. While optimized for desktop, basic viewing works on tablets. Full funnel building is best done on a larger screen.
